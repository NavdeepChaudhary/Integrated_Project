Kerala : "God's Own Country"

Kerala is a beautiful state in South India known for its lush green landscapes, backwaters, beaches, and cultural heritage. People often call it "God's Own Country" because of its stunning beauty.

Top Destinations In Kerala

Kochi

  • Fort Kochi : Fort Kochi is situated along the Arabian Sea, and its beach is a popular spot for both tourists and locals.Princess Street is a lively street in Fort Kochi known for its colonial-style buildings, boutiques, art galleries, and cafes.
  • St. Francis Church :It is one of the oldest European churches in India,where explorer Vasco da Gama was originally buried.The current structure reflects a mix of architectural styles, including Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences.
  • Cherai Beach : Cherai Beach is a beautiful and peaceful destination, perfect for those seeking a relaxing beach experience away from the hustle and bustle.Cherai Beach is known for the abundance of seashells.

    • Munnar

  • Tea Gardens :

    Munnar is renowned for its extensive tea gardens and panoramic views of the lush green plantations.

  • Eravikulam National Park :

    Eravikulam National Park is known for its rolling hills, grasslands, and diverse flora. The park is also famous for the Neelakurinji flowers that bloom once every 12 years.

  • Attukal Waterfalls :

    A scenic waterfall surrounded by hills and lush greenery. The area is perfect for trekking and nature walks.

    • Thekkady

  • Periyar National Park :

    The sanctuary offers boat cruises on the Periyar Lake, providing opportunities to spot wildlife.

  • Mangla Devi Temple :

    Located in the dense forests of Thekkady ,dedicated to Mangala Devi offers a trekking trail through the wilderness.

  • Parunthumpara Viewpoint :

    It is a spot for photography and known for its eagle-shaped rock formations and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills

    • Wayand

  • Soochipara Waterfalls :

    Soochipara is a three-tiered waterfall surrounded by dense forests. The waterfall cascades down from a height of around 200 meters, creating a breathtaking spectacle. Visitors can trek through the forest to reach the base of the falls

  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary :

    Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is home to diverse flora and fauna. It's a part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve.The sanctuary offers jeep safaris and trekking opportunities for visitors

  • Edakkal Caves :

    These natural caves are famous for their ancient rock engravings.The cave walls have carvings depicting human and animal figures, along with symbols.To reach the caves,you have to do a scenic trek.

    • Allepey

  • Alleppey Beach :

    It is a beautiful strech of golden sand along Arabian Sea. It's a popular spot for beach lovers to enjoy sea offering a relaxing ambiance.

  • Allepey backwaters :

    The backwaters of Alleppey are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, and lakes, offering a unique and tranquil experience. Houseboat cruises are a popular way to explore the backwaters

  • Kumarakom :

    Kumarakom is famous for its backwaters, bird sanctuary, and luxury resorts.You can explore the serene backwaters, go bird watching at the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary.

    • Varkala

  • Varkala Beach :

    Varkala Beach is a beautiful stretch of golden sands bordered by towering cliffs.You can take in the panoramic views from the cliffs and enjoy preace here.

  • Varkala Cliff :

    he North Cliff of Varkala, and ideal place for sunset offers breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea. The cliff is dotted with cafes, shops, and accommodations.

  • Janardanaswamy Temple :

    A historic Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple is known for its architectural beauty and religious significance.
