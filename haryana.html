Haryana : "Land of Milk and Butter."

Haryana, located in northern India, boasts a rich historical legacy dating back to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization. Renowned for its fertile plains, the state is a major agricultural hub, contributing significantly to India's food grain production.

Top Destinations In Haryana

Gurugram

  • Cyber Hub : Cyber Hub is a vibrant entertainment and dining destination in Gurugram. It features a plethora of restaurants, cafes, bars, and entertainment venues. Visitors can enjoy a diverse range of cuisines and nightlife experiences in this lively hub.
  • Worldmark :Worldmark Gurugram is a premier mixed-use development located in Sector 65, Gurugram, India. It is part of the larger Worldmark complex developed by Bharti Realty Limited.Is a mixed-use development that combines commercial office spaces, retail outlets, dining options, and entertainment facilities in a modern and vibrant environment.
  • Ambience Mall : Ambience Mall is one of the largest shopping malls in India, offering a wide array of retail outlets, restaurants, entertainment options, and recreational facilities. Visitors can indulge in shopping from leading national and international brands, catch a movie at the multiplex, or enjoy various indoor activities.

    • Faridabad

  • Surajkund :

    Surajkund is an ancient reservoir and tourist complex located in Faridabad. It is famous for its annual Surajkund Crafts Mela, which showcases traditional Indian handicrafts, arts, and cultural performances. The mela attracts artisans and visitors from all over the country and abroad.

  • Badkhal Lake :

    Badkhal Lake, located in Badkhal village near Faridabad, was once a natural lake surrounded by hills. Although the lake has dried up over the years, the area still attracts visitors for its scenic beauty and serene ambiance. It's a great place for nature walks, birdwatching, and enjoying panoramic views of the Aravalli hills.

  • Raja Nahar Singh Palace :

    Raja Nahar Singh Palace is a historical landmark in Faridabad, known for its architectural grandeur and cultural significance. Built in the 19th century, the palace served as the residence of Raja Nahar Singh, a prominent Jat ruler.

    • Hisar

  • Hisar Fort :

    Hisar Fort, also known as Prithvi Raj Chauhan Fort, is a prominent historical landmark in Hisar. Built during the medieval period by Emperor Firoz Shah Tughlaq, the fort has witnessed various historical events.

  • Feroze Shah's Palace Complex :

    Located within the premises of Hisar Fort, the Feroze Shah's Palace Complex is another notable attraction. It comprises a series of structures, including the Diwan-e-Aam (Hall of Public Audience) and the Diwan-e-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), built during the Tughlaq dynasty.

  • Agroha Dham :

    Agroha Dham is a revered pilgrimage site located near Hisar. It is associated with the Aggarwal community and is dedicated to the ancient deity Goddess Mahalakshmi.

    • Panipat

  • Panipat Battlefield Sites :

    Panipat is famous for its historical battlefield sites, which witnessed three major battles that had significant impacts on Indian history. These battles include the First Battle of Panipat (1526), the Second Battle of Panipat (1556), and the Third Battle of Panipat (1761).

  • Kabuli Bagh Mosque :

    Built by the Mughal emperor Babur in the 16th century, the Kabuli Bagh Mosque is a notable architectural landmark in Panipat. The mosque is renowned for its Afghan architectural style and intricate designs.

  • Panipat Museum :

    The Panipat Museum is dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of the region, with a particular focus on the three battles of Panipat. The museum features a wide range of artifacts, weapons, documents, and multimedia exhibits related to the battles and their aftermath.

    • Kurushetra

  • Brahma Sarovar :

    Brahma Sarovar is one of the largest and holiest water tanks in Kurukshetra. According to Hindu mythology, it is believed that Lord Brahma created the universe at this site. The tank is surrounded by ghats and temples.

  • Jyotisar :

    Jyotisar is a revered pilgrimage site associated with the Bhagavad Gita. It is believed that Lord Krishna delivered the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna at this spot during the Mahabharata war.

  • Kurukshetra Panorama and Science Centre :

    This museum offers an immersive experience into the epic Mahabharata and the historical significance of Kurukshetra. The Panorama depicts scenes from the Mahabharata on a large cylindrical canvas, providing visitors with a visual narrative of the epic .

    • Karnal

  • Karnal Fort :

    Also known as the Old Fort or the Karna Fort, this historical landmark is located in the heart of Karnal. Believed to have been built by Raja Gajpat Singh of the Sisodia dynasty in the 18th century, the fort showcases traditional Indian architecture and offers a glimpse into the city's past.

  • Kalpana Chawla Memorial Planetarium :

    Named in honor of Kalpana Chawla, the first woman of Indian origin in space who tragically perished in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster, this planetarium is a popular attraction in Karnal. It features interactive exhibits, informative displays, and regular astronomy shows aimed at educating visitors about space science and exploration.

  • Cantonment Church Tower :

    Situated in Karnal Cantonment, this historic structure is a remnant of British colonial architecture in the region. The church tower stands as a silent witness to Karnal's colonial past and serves as a prominent landmark in the city.
