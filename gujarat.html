Ahmedabad - The Heritage City

  • Sabarmati Ashram :

    This serene retreat was once the home of Mahatma Gandhi, the father of the Indian nation. It's a place of great historical significance where visitors can learn about Gandhi's life, philosophy.

  • Sidi Saiyyed Mosque :

    Famous for its intricate stone lattice-work windows (jalis), the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque is an architectural masterpiece dating back to the 16th century .

  • Adalaj Stepwell :

    Located on the outskirts of Ahmedabad, the Adalaj Stepwell is a stunning example of traditional Indian architecture. Built in the 15th century, this intricately carved stepwell served as a source of water and a place for social gatherings .

    • Surat

  • Dutch Garden :

    This historical garden is a remnant of Surat's colonial past and is named after the Dutch settlers who once resided here. It features a quaint cemetery with elaborately carved tombs and graves of Dutch traders dating back to the 17th century.

  • Surat Castle :

    Built during the reign of Khudawand Khan, the Surat Castle is a significant historical monument in the city.

  • Sardar Patel Museum :

    Named after India's first Deputy Prime Minister, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, this museum showcases a diverse collection of artifacts related to Surat's history, culture, and archaeology .

    • Vadodara

  • Laxmi Vilas Palace :

    This magnificent palace is one of the grandest structures in India and was the residence of the Gaekwads, the former rulers of Vadodara. Built-in the Indo-Saracenic style, the palace boasts opulent interiors, sprawling gardens, and a remarkable collection of artifacts .

  • Sayaji Baug (Kamati Baug) :

    Sayaji Baug is a sprawling garden and recreational park located in the heart of Vadodara. It was built by Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III in the late 19th century and is named after him .

  • Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park :

    While technically located outside Vadodara, Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site situated around 50 kilometers from the city.

    • Rajkot

  • Watson Museum and Library :

    Named after Colonel John Watson, the British political agent of Kathiawad, this museum is a treasure trove of artifacts, sculptures, coins, and manuscripts. It showcases the rich cultural heritage of Gujarat, including exhibits related to history, archaeology, and natural history.

  • Jubilee Garden (Race Course Ground):

    Spread over a vast area, Jubilee Garden is a popular recreational space in Rajkot. It features lush green lawns, colorful flower beds, and various attractions like a children's park, amusement rides, and boating facilities .

  • Kaba Gandhi No Delo (Gandhi's Childhood Home):

    This historic residence was the ancestral home of Mahatma Gandhi's family and is now converted into a museum dedicated to his life and legacy .

    • Jamnagar

  • Lakhota Fort and Lakhota Lake:

    Lakhota Fort is a 19th-century fortress located on an island in the middle of Lakhota Lake. The fort houses a museum with exhibits on local history, artifacts, and sculptures.

  • Bala Hanuman Temple:

    This temple is famous for its continuous chanting of the "Ram Dhun" since August 1, 1964, earning it a place in the Guinness Book of World Records.

  • Marine National Park and Pirotan Island:

    Located off the coast of Jamnagar, Marine National Park is India's first marine sanctuary and offers opportunities for eco-tourism and marine conservation. Pirotan Island, within the park, is known for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and diverse marine life.

    • Bhavnagar

  • Takhteshwar Temple:

    Perched on a small hill overlooking the city, Takhteshwar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Besides its religious significance, the temple offers panoramic views of Bhavnagar and its surroundings, making it a popular spot for both worship and sightseeing.

  • Nilambagh Palace:

    Built-in the 19th century, Nilambagh Palace is an architectural gem that reflects the grandeur of the bygone era. Initially a royal residence, it has been converted into a heritage hotel.

  • Gaurishankar Lake and Victoria Park:

    Gaurishankar Lake, surrounded by Victoria Park, is a serene oasis in the heart of Bhavnagar. The park offers lush greenery, walking paths, and boating facilities, providing a peaceful retreat for locals and tourists alike.
