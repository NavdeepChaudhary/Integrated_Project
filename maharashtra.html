Maharashtra : "Land of Marathas"

Maharashtra , situated in western India is known for its rich cultural heritage.Home to the bustling metropolis of Mumbai, the financial and entertainment capital of the country, Maharashtra offers a blend of historical significance and contemporary vibrancy.

Top Destinations In Maharashtra

Mumbai

  • Gateway of India : The Gateway of India stands tall as a symbol of historical significance.It was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911 and is located at the Apollo Bunder waterfront.It is also a starting point for boat trips to Elephanta Island.
  • Marine Drive :It is known as the "Queen's Necklace" due to its graceful curve along the Arabian Sea.It is a popular spot offering a peaceful escape, stunning sunsets, and a leisurely stroll along the coastline with sparkling city lights at night.
  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT) : Also known as Victoria Terminus , this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a historic railway station that showcases stunning Victorian Gothic architecture. It is a visual treat with its intricate carvings, grand domes, and impressive detailing.

    • Pune

  • Shaniwar Wada :

    Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification and palace that served as the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire.It includes massive agtes,gardens and a large hall known as Diwan-i-Khas.It is a must visit historical site.

  • Aga Khan Palace :

    It was built by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III, the Aga Khan Palace became famous as a prison where Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba, and his secretary Mahadev Desai were held during the Quit India Movement.Palace is surrounded by lush greenery and also houses a meusem dedicated to Ganshi's life.

  • Shinde Chhatri :

    Shinde Chhatri is known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful surroundings. The memorial houses a temple and a memorial hall with intricate carvings.It is a memorial dedicated to a military leader Mahadji Shinde.

    • Nashik

  • Sula Vineyards :

    Sula Vineyards stands as a leading winery in the area.Visitors can learn about the winemaking process, and enjoy wine tastings. The scenic landscape adds to the charm, making it a popular destination for wine enthusiasts and those looking for a relaxed experience.

  • Trimbakeshwar Shiva Temple :

    Trimbakeshwar is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India and attracts pilgrims from across the country.It is located about 28 kilometers from Nashik.It a must-visit destination for those interested in religious experiences.

    • Aurangabad

  • Ajanta Caves :

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Ajanta Caves are a series of rock-cut Buddhist caves and and a must-visit destination for art enthusiasts.These caves are renowned for their exquisite murals and sculptures depicting the life of Buddha and various Buddhist stories.

  • Bibi ka Maqbara :

    It is also referred to as the "Taj of the Deccan".It is built by Emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his wife, Dilras Banu Begum. The structure resembles the iconic Taj Mahal, featuring a large marble dome and intricate carvings.

  • Ellora Caves :

    Ellora Caves are a complex of rock-cut temples and monasteries representing Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism.Ellora Caves showcase remarkable architectural diversity and craftsmanship. The Kailasa Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is particularly famous for its grandeur and scale.

    • Mahabaleshwar

  • Arthur's Seat :

    Arthur's Seat is a prominent viewpoint that offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills. It is the highest point in Mahabaleshwar, and on clear days, one can see the Savitri River winding through the lush landscapes.

  • Venna Lake :

    Venna Lake is a picturesque artificial lake surrounded by lush greenery and hills. Visitors can enjoy boating on the lake, and there are also horse rides and other recreational activities available along its shores.

  • Pratapgarh Fort :

    Pratapgarh Fort is a historic hill fort built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century. The fort offers a blend of historical significance and scenic beauty, with panoramic views of the surrounding hills and forests. The fort has a Bhavani Temple and the tomb of Afzal Khan.

    • Nagpur

  • Deekshabhoomi :

    Deekshabhoomi is a sacred monument and the largest hollow stupa in the world. It holds immense significance in Buddhism as it is the place where Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, the architect of the Indian Constitution, converted to Buddhism.

  • Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve :

    For nature and wildlife enthusiasts, Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve is a must-visit destination. It is one of the oldest and largest national parks in Maharashtra, known for its rich biodiversity, including the Bengal tiger, leopards, sloth bears, and various species of deer and birds.

  • Sitabuldi Fort :

    Sitabuldi Fort is a historical fortress that played a role in the First Anglo-Maratha War. The fort provides panoramic views of Nagpur and its surroundings.It is popular spot with markets,cafes,and cultural activities.
